Step 1: The "5-Minute" Golden Rule

The single most important factor in winning a new job is speed. This section breaks down why every second counts and what you must do to get ahead of your competition instantly.

How Response Time Impacts Your Chance to Win a Job

Respond to every single website enquiry in under 5 minutes.
The first roofer to respond is statistically the most likely to win the job. Homeowners want speed. Being first makes you seem the most professional and eager for their business.
Set up instant email and SMS notifications from your website form so you or your team are alerted the second an enquiry comes in.

Step 2: The "Triple Touch" First Contact

Once you get a lead, making immediate and effective contact is crucial. A multi-channel approach ensures your message is received and positions you as a proactive professional.

Contact the new lead via three different methods immediately.
People have preferred communication styles. Covering all bases ensures they see your message.
  • Send an Automated Email: "Thanks for your quote request for [Their Address]. We're reviewing the details and will call you in the next 5-10 minutes..."
  • Send an Automated SMS: "Hi [Name], just got your roofing quote request. Roger from [Your Company] here. I'll call you shortly from this number."
  • Make the Phone Call: Call them within 5-10 minutes.

Step 3: The "Certainty" Quote Package

Your quote is your most important sales document. It must do more than just show a price—it must build confidence, eliminate risk, and make the homeowner feel certain that you are the right choice.

Your quote should do more than just list a price; it should build trust and eliminate risk for the homeowner.
Homeowners are scared of being ripped off. A professional package makes your price seem more justified and makes you look like the safest choice.

Always send your quote as a PDF that includes:

  • Your Company Details & Logo
  • The Client's Details
  • A Clear Scope of Work
  • Proof of Insurance
  • Photos of Past Work / Customer Testimonials

Step 4: The "No-Brainer" Follow-Up Sequence

Most jobs are lost due to a lack of follow-up. A simple, systematic, and non-pushy follow-up sequence keeps you top-of-mind and shows the client you are organized and attentive.

Follow up systematically without being annoying.
Life gets in the way. Most roofers never follow up. A simple, helpful follow-up sequence keeps you top of mind.

If you don't hear back, follow this schedule:

  • Day 2 (SMS): "Hi [Name], just checking you received the quote we sent yesterday. Let me know if you have any questions. Thanks, [Your Name]."
  • Day 4 (Email): "Subject: Any questions about your roofing quote?" - Briefly re-attach the quote and ask if they need any clarification.
  • Day 7 (Phone Call): One final, friendly call to see if they've made a decision.

Step 5: The "Lost Lead" Revival Email

Even when you lose a quote, there's a valuable opportunity to learn and potentially recover the job. This final step improves your process for the future and keeps the door open.

Get feedback even if you lose the job.
This provides valuable market intelligence and occasionally revives a dead lead if their first choice falls through.
If you know they went with someone else, send one last email: "Hi [Name], thanks for the opportunity to quote. For our records, would you mind sharing what the deciding factor was? Your feedback is incredibly helpful. All the best with the project."
